What we noticed

Your current site is working against you.

We audited pdtowingofhouston.com from a property manager's perspective. Seven things stood out — none of them are the fault of your operation, all of them are fixable.

The phone number isn't tappable

On a phone, a property manager has to copy your number by hand to call you. For a 24/7 emergency business, that's the #1 conversion crime.

Looks like it was built in 2012

The Joomla template literally hasn't been updated since the copyright notice — 2012. Property managers see that footer and assume you've gone out of business.

No social proof

Fifteen years of work and not a single review, testimonial, or property logo on the site. To a manager comparing tow vendors, that reads as "no one's vouched for them."

No mobile design

The site renders at desktop width on a phone. Property managers (who live on their phones) have to pinch-zoom to read it. Most leave instead.

Your email is hidden

Both emails are blocked behind "JavaScript anti-spam" — fine in 2012, but today every property manager browses with privacy tools that strip that out. They never see your email.

No CTA — anywhere

No "Request a site audit." No "Get a quote." No way for a property manager to express interest. The funnel ends at static contact info.

The value you're already delivering is invisible

You photograph every tow. You notify the police line in real time. You email invoices with photos within minutes. You're TDLR-compliant. That's a B2B-grade operation — but nowhere on the site does a property manager see a hint of it. The site is hiding the best part of what PD does.

Current site grade: 2 out of 10

The operation is 9 out of 10. The website is the bottleneck.
